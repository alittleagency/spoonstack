Built on spoon theory

People with chronic illness or neurodivergent brains start each day with a limited pool of energy. Different tasks cost different amounts. Healthy people don't count spoons because they never run out.

We count because we have to.

The hard stuff still costs spoons.
The overhead shouldn't.

The philosophy

You are the CEO.
SpoonStack is your
chief of staff.

Claude never decides for you. It reduces the cost of deciding - so your actual decision-making capacity goes to the things that matter. Human-led. AI-assisted. Always.

Decision fatigue is the real enemy
AuDHD brains make hundreds more micro-decisions per day than neurotypical brains - because nothing is automatic. SpoonStack removes the decision overhead from each task so your tank isn't empty when it matters.
Executive dysfunction is not a lack of intelligence
The gap between knowing what to do and being able to do it is executive function. SpoonStack bridges that gap - the first step is clearer, the words are already written, the thing stuck in your head is on the screen. You still do the thing.
"Your brain doesn't need to work harder.
It needs fewer decisions."
